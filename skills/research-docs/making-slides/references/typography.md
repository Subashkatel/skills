# Typography rules for slides and documents

Distilled from Robert Bringhurst, *The Elements of Typographic Style* (2nd ed.) — section numbers in parentheses — and Oskar Wickström, *The Proportional Web* (owickstrom.github.io/the-proportional-web). PDF copies of both live in the qlx-qec-sandbox repo root on the research cluster.

## Scale

- Don't compose without a scale (3.1.1). Traditional series: 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 14, 16, 18, 21, 24, 36.
- Proportional Web variant: fractions of a root size — ¾, ⅞, 1, 9/8, 5/4, 11/8, 3/2, 2, 5/2, 3, 4.
- Slide scale in use (root 12): 10.5, 12, 13.5, 15, 18, 24, 30, 36 pt. Pick sizes only from the scale.

## Rhythm

- Choose a basic leading and treat it as the rhythmic unit (2.2.1); slides use 1.2.
- Add and delete vertical space in measured intervals — multiples of the leading (2.2.2); Proportional Web aligns everything to the line-height grid.
- Don't suffocate the page (2.2.3). White space is poise; poise beats size (4.1.1).

## Measure

- 45–75 characters per line, 66 ideal; 40–50 for multiple columns (2.1.2).

## Color

- Black on white by default; reserve color for the most critical content — diagrams conveying data (Proportional Web §1.2).
- Text wears ink; a colored mark next to it carries identity. Never repaint a label in the series color.

## Hierarchy and emphasis

- Change one parameter at a time: size OR weight OR posture (3.5.1). For balance, weight should decrease slightly as size increases.
- Don't clutter the foreground (3.5.2); punctuation stays in the background weight.
- As many heading levels as needed, no more (4.2.2). Don't restate the obvious (8.5.4).
- Use bold sparingly: flag list items, small subheads — never to shout (3.4.2).

## Letters, numbers, punctuation

- Letterspace all strings of caps/small caps and long digit strings 5–10% of size; 20–25% in titles (2.1.6). Never letterspace lowercase without reason (2.1.7).
- Spaced small caps for acronyms in running text (3.2.2). Text figures with lowercase, titling figures with full caps (3.2.1).
- Single word space after periods (2.1.4). Close-set en dashes between digits: 1640–1842 (5.2.2); spaced en dashes — like this — for phrases (5.2.1).
- Dimension sign × (not letter x) for dimensions (5.2.6). Hard spaces inside "6.2 mm", "page 3" (2.4.6).
- Lists: flush right or decimal-align columns of figures (4.4.3); no dot leaders or chasms of space (2.1.10).

## Page and proportion

- Choose satisfying page/textblock proportions over arbitrary shapes (8.3.1). Musical ratios: 1:1 unison, 4:5 major 3rd, 2:3 fifth, 1:√2 (self-replicating), 1:1.618 golden (~minor 6th), 9:16 minor 7th (the 16:9 slide!), 1:2 octave.
- Textblock balances AND contrasts the page shape (8.4.2); columns clearly taller than wide invite continuous reading (8.4.1).
- Bring margins into the design; margins lock the textblock to the page (8.5.1). Half the character of a page is its letterforms, much of the rest its margins.
- Use a modular scale (e.g. double-stranded Fibonacci on 1:φ and 1:2) to subdivide the page (8.6.1); a scale on two ratios beats one.
- Typographic increments inside the textblock; free proportion for the empty space (8.8.2). Improvise, calculate, improvise some more (8.8.1).

## Faces

- Start with a single typographic family (6.5.1); choose a face that suits the task and honors the text (1.2.4, 6.2.1).
- If the text will be read on screen, design it for the screen (9.5.1).
